But it's only a piece of paper

  • One Page
  • Font: easy to read, 10pt minimum
  • Clear titles and headings
  • Anything is game for conversation.
  • List skills in order of proficiency.
  • List any relevant links: LinkedIn, portfolio, Github, personal website, research profile, etc.

Exception to the Norm: Yes, it's all about you

  • Project: "I accomplished X, relative to Y, by doing Z using technology T, language L, ..."
  • Research: "I [developed, analyzed, ran experiments, etc] [on] X to see the effect of Y on Z in order to see if [insert research purpose]."
  • Strong Verbs
  • Bring out the numbers! "I initiated the use of X company-wide by streamlining process Y, which led to savings of Z per fiscal year"
  • Sophisticated layman's terms.

It's on my Resume, why ask me about it again?

  • "Tell me about yourself." = Summary of your resume
  • Show clear communication skills.
  • STAR Method: Situation, Task, Action, Result
  • Prepare to talk about challenges and how you overcame them.
  • Eye contact, no fidgety habits
  • It's OK to pause a second before speaking.

Gotta have those Cheerios

  • Experiment/Knowledge Questions: Uses, effects, applications help boost the definition.
  • Design/Case Study Questions: Identify the key issues first, prioritize.
  • Present your solution in a logical, coherent way - "First step, I would do X because it can improve Y through Z."
  • It's all about the customer.
  • Bonus: Relate back to the company's motivations.

Whiteboard + Marker + code + syntax that I wish I had Google for

  • Pick the language that you are most comfortable with.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for more context/information.
  • Don’t say silent for too long.
  • Walk the interviewer through your thought process and get feedback first.
  • “First thing that comes to mind…”
  • Optimize after completion.
  • Explain in depth how and why you chose your solution.

Big-O seeped into my brain

  • What data structures can help solve this problem?
  • What common algorithms can I employ?
  • What is the runtime of my solution? How can I decrease the complexity?
  • If I use X sort or search, what is its runtime? Why is it so?
  • What are some corner cases that I have to consider?

It's not just about them, it's about you too

  • Research the company before hand.
  • Always have a question to ask.
  • Does this role fit your interests?
  • Does the company support your learning and career?
  • Doe you think the company culture fits you?
